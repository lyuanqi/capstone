Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) Will Easily Meet Its Delivery Target For Q4: Trip Chowdhry

Published By at December 6, 2016 08:54 am Analyst Trip Chowdhry believes that delivering 25,000 cars in the current quarter won't be a problem for Tesla

Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA), over time, has been criticized by many due to its failure to deliver cars on time. The company rarely meets its delivery targets. But in its third quarter of the current fiscal year, the electric car maker did meet its delivery target which raised hopes amongst a lot of customers to receive their vehicles on time.

And now Trip Chowdhry, analyst at Global Equities Research, in a note to investors, said that the company would be easily achieving its delivery targets for the fourth quarter of the current fiscal year. Trip Chowdhry has had an Overweight rating on Tesla stock with a target price of $385 since summer 2015.

Mr. Chowdhry believes that Tesla will be facing no issues in the current quarter and will manage to deliver 25,000 cars. It will be a 50% mix for both the Tesla cars that are currently on the street i.e. Model X and Model S. It is expected that the company’s much-awaited low-cost Model 3 will hit the roads early next year.

The 50% mix of both the cars will help the Average Selling Price (ASP) of the Palo Alto company in the quarter. With the company delivering 90% of its Model S with glass roof option and 60% with the Autopilot 2.0 option, it will have a good chance to improve its ASP in the quarter.

While meeting the delivery target for the quarter is a good sign for Tesla, Trip Chowdhry says that the demand for the electric cars is more than the production. This can turn out to be a potential problem for the company in the coming years if it is unable to meet its demand and deliver products on time.

Tesla, in the same quarter last year, delivered 14,037 vehicles, which means that it will be increasing its delivery capacity by more than 78% on a year-over-year (YoY) basis. The electric car maker has worked hard to achieve its delivery targets with continuous investments in the Gigafactory and other projects.

The EV maker recently also acquired German engineering firm, Grohmann Engineering, to help boost the production of Model 3. The first year of Model 3 production has already been declared as sold out by Elon Musk which shows that Tesla will need to increase its production capacity and speed to meet future demand.
