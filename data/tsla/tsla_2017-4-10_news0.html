The $9 Billion Bet Against Tesla Stock That Is Going Wrong, For Now

Posted by Kumar Abhishek on April 10, 2017, 06:57 AM EST
Related Tickers: TSLA , F , GM

The rising skepticism against Tesla Inc has led to a huge build-up of short interest in TSLA stock.


Flickr

The U.S auto sector had a pretty bad start to April. Industrywide deliveries slowed last month to a Seasonally Adjusted Annual Rate (SAAR) of 16.6 million vehicles against analyst expectations of 17.2 million. This has strengthened the argument that the U.S automobile sector is staring at a cyclical downturn due to rising interest rates, rising negative equity in vehicle loans and used vehicle price deflation.

On the other hand, the manufacturer of luxury electric cars Tesla Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) continued to surprise its skeptics. Tesla delivered a record 25,000 vehicles in the first quarter, good for 69% YoY growth, which was better than analysts' estimates (a not so frequent event for Tesla). And as expected Tesla stock rallied on this news, breaking out above the $300 mark for the first time. Tesla Inc stock has been on a bull run for some time now with surprisingly cordial relations with the current administration, positive news on its Gigafactory and Model 3 acting as catalysts. Tesla Inc stock has rallied more than 66% since the beginning of December last year. In comparison, the Nasdaq Composite (INDX:COMPX) has delivered 10% return, while Ford (NYSE:F) stock has declined by almost 10% in the same period.

Source: Tesla Inc Stock Price Chart by amigobulls.com

Tesla Inc stock got another boost when the company announced that it had raised around $1.15 billion ($250 million of equity shares and raised $750 million through convertible notes maturing in 2022) which will help in reducing the company's liquidity risk. As we have discussed earlier, Tesla Inc needs a massive amount of cash for operations and investment.

The $9 billion bet against Tesla Inc.

However, in spite of the recent positive news, the bears continue to be skeptical about TSLA stock. High valuation, the huge need for cash and the execution risks are the main concerns raised by Tesla bears. Tesla Inc recently became more valuable than Ford and General Motors (NYSE:GM). For comparison, Tesla Inc lost $773 million last year while Ford reported a profit of $11 billion and GM profits came in at $9.4 billion. Of course, the stock price is the discounted cash flow of future earnings hence past performance may not be a true indicator of companies value. However, given Tesla's current production volume and the risks ahead, many analysts argue that Tesla stock is highly overvalued even if you take an optimistic view of the future. Recently Barclays analyst Brian Johnson opined that Tesla Inc bulls are living in a "real life Matrix" and are blind to the reality that the company faces several significant challenges.

The rising skepticism against Tesla has led to a huge build-up of short interest in TSLA stock. More than 31 million Tesla shares currently remain shorted, making up 25% of the company's float. The $9 billion bet is the largest "short" position in U.S. markets in terms of dollars at risk. According to Ihor Dusaniwsky, head of research at S3 Partners "Its one of the biggest shorts ever". According to him "If theres $8.6 billion of market money shorting [the stock], there is a serious amount of conviction that something is up with Tesla,". However, shorts have lost huge money in past. Tesla Inc stock has rallied 790% in last five years. the recent bullish rally in the stock will add more salt to the wound. Even Elon Musk, Tesla CEO took a dig at shorts after the stock rallied on delivery announcement tweeting "Stormy weather in Shortville ...".

Tesla Inc stock remains a risky bet.

Given that Tesla stock is currently trading at 112 times its future earnings and the challenges ahead, the stock is definitely a risky investment. However, given the fact that Tesla Inc stock is currently enjoying a strong momentum, it might be risky to bet against TSLA stock for now. Even Brian Johnson, who rates Tesla Underweight with a $165 price target doesnt see any upcoming data that will easily reverse the momentum in Tesla Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) stock. Tesla stock will be driven by the news on Model 3 front and any good news could drive Tesla stock much higher.
