Tesla Motors | $TSLA Stock | Company Quick To Defend Its Suspension System

By Sean V -
June 10, 2016
0
864
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter

Tesla Motors (NASDAQ: TSLA)

 

 

Tesla Motors News & Technicals

On Thursday June 9th Tesla Motors published a blog defending its suspension systems for its Model S and Model X in response to a negative post that stated their was a defect in the suspensions based on complaints from customers, whether true or not. The company was quick to issue a response and fight off the claims and even though the shares haven’t declined too much its best for companies to squash this type of chatter as quick as possible especially when you have nearly 33% of your float in the hands of short sellers. We’ll have to wait and see where this story goes but keep in mind as a trader its best not to trade the news but rather focus on the price action.

Looking at the chart you will see that shares have been on a nice rebound after sharply bouncing off the $140 level back in February with shares surging all the way back above $260. Shares have come back off those recent highs but were able to put in a higher low and maintain their 200-day moving average currently sitting at $221.46. There looks to be a wedge forming on the daily chart which will result in a breakout to one side or the other but currently it looks like were going back down to the lower end of the wedge with the help of current market conditions and this news looming over TSLA shares. As of Thursday’s close at $229.36 shares are down half a percent for the year.

 

Tesla’s Comments

“First, there is no safety defect with the suspensions in either the Model S or Model X. Since we own all of our service centers, we are aware of every incident that happens with our customer cars and we are aware of every part that gets replaced. Whenever there is even a potential issue with one of those parts, we investigate fully. This, combined with extensive durability testing, gives us high confidence in our suspensions. With respect to the car that is discussed in the blog post that led to yesterday’s news (more on the blog post below), the suspension ball joint experienced very abnormal rust. We haven’t seen this on any other car, suggesting a very unusual use case. The car had over 70,000 miles on it and its owner lives down such a long dirt road that it required two tow trucks to retrieve the car. (One to get the car to the highway and one to get it from the highway to the service center.) When we got the car, it was caked in dirt. . . “

“Finally, it is worth noting that the blogger who fabricated this issue, which then caused negative and incorrect news to be written about Tesla by reputable institutions, is Edward Niedermayer. This is the same gentle soul who previously wrote a blog titled “Tesla Death Watch,” which starting on May 19, 2008 was counting the days until Tesla’s death. It has now been 2,944 days. We just checked our pulse and, much to his chagrin, appear to be alive. It is probably wise to take Mr. Niedermayer’s words with at least a small grain of salt.” – TESLA

 

TSLA Profile

Tesla Motors, Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles and stationary energy storage products in the United States, China, Norway, and internationally. It primarily offers sedans and sport utility vehicles. The company also offers electric vehicle powertrain components and systems to other manufacturers. Tesla Motors, Inc. sells its products through a network of Tesla stores and galleries, as well as through Internet. The company was founded in 2003 and is headquartered in Palo Alto, California. (YahooFinance)
