Tesla Inc (TSLA) Share Price Will Increase 10 Times in the Next 5-10 Years

Published By News Desk at April 23, 2017 08:04 am The increase can make Tesla the largest automaker in the world

Tesla Inc. (NASDAQ:TSLA) stock has been rallying up for the past two months, and it hit an all-time high of $312 per share. It was also the most valuable US automobile company by passing the likes of General Motors and Ford. This came as a surprise to many people, but CEO Elon Musk thinks otherwise and says that this is only the beginning for Tesla to become one of the most valuable companies in the world.

According to a report by Electrek, Musk predicts that Tesla share price will increase by tenfold in the next five to ten years, just like it increased tenfold in the previous five years. And if the company manages to achieve this feat in the next five to ten years, then it will be competing with the likes of Apple, Google, and Facebook that are currently the most valuable companies in the world.

On the basis of the current share price of the company, a tenfold increase will take Tesla’s valuation to around $500 billion which will be only less than Apple and Google that have a market capitalization in excess of $500. Facebook and Amazon are currently around $400 billion. Not only this, but Tesla will also become the most valuable automaker around the world.

And that too by some margin as the largest automaker in the world right now is Toyota with a market cap of $175 billion. If the Palo Alto Company achieves its target of $500 billion, then it will be 2-3 times more valuable than Toyota. The company won’t be the only one to gain from this increase, Elon Musk will also gain a lot if the price increases by ten folds in the future.

Musk currently has 22% stake in the company, and the price increase could make him the richest man on the planet due to his stake. Tesla has also been offering stock compensation to its employees because the company believes that in the next 5-6 years it will be a force to reckon and employees can then take advantage of those stock options.
