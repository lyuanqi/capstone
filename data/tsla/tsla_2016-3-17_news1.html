Did A Tesla Motors (TSLA) Employee Just Leak Model 3 Mockup Pic?

March 17, 2016 By Aman Jain 2 Comments Filed Under: Automotive, News, Technology

Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) will soon launch its much-awaited Model 3. But, we already have a leaked image of what the car will possibly look like.  An anonymous person claiming to be a Tesla employee sent a message from a Gmail account to Jalopnik. The email included an image that is claimed to be Tesla crossover – Model 3.

Is it Model 3 or Model Y – or Nothing?

“I work at Tesla. I can’t stand the company and the management. Yeah, they’re doing good things, but they treat us like shit. I was able to access the attached image on my boss’ computer. This was risky as hell! The image quality sucks, but it’s better than nothing. Enjoy…,” the email reads.

However, the guys at Jalopnik doubt whether the car in the image is a Model Y or a Model 3 or any other Tesla for that matter, but the image cannot be avoided. It is important to remember that the new Model 3 platform will actually generate two cars and not just one $35000 Model 3 that the people are most excited about.

The two cars being Model 3 sedan, the one we will probably see in a few weeks, and a crossover/SUV-type variant similar to the Model X—possibly called the Model Y, says Jalopnik. The image is already making rounds on the internet as a “leaked image” though many doubt its credibility.

This anonymous person, to prove that he/she is a Tesla employee, sent an entire confidential employee directory, complete with the phone numbers and emails for more than 800 people. As one cannot easily get hold of such content, it’s appears likely that the sender has some direct connection or access to the electric car firm.

By doing a bit of Google search, Jalopnik found the image to be a very common Model X press photo. But the source claimed “Not a model X. It’s smaller by 18%. Compare for yourself (See attached). There will be multiple variants of the III. This is the crossover. As Elon said, its not going to look like other cars, which is clearly an understatement.”

When Jalopnik asked Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) about it, the electric carmaker stressed that it’s not a Tesla image, and has not come from them. And, Jalopnik is not the only outlet to get this email, and the car in the image is not the Model 3. The automaker agrees that the car appears to be a Photo shopped Model X press picture.

Lawmakers tour Tesla Gigafactory

On Wednesday, ten state lawmakers toured Tesla’s under-construction Gigafactory east of Reno. The lucky lawmakers also got the opportunity to meet the CEO Elon Musk and his cousin Lyndon Rive, who is the founder of the rooftop solar company SolarCity.

The lawmakers, representing both parties and both the Assembly and Senate were present, including Assembly Majority Leader Paul Anderson; R-Las Vegas, and Senate Minority Leader Aaron Ford. The visitors also included Steve Hill – the executive director of the Governor’s Office of Economic Development, but the media representatives were not invited. Leonardo DiCaprio, recent Oscar winner and a climate change adherent was also part of the event.

The lawmakers after the tour heard a vision of Nevada’s energy future that Rive suggested must include a net metering policy to lower the reliance on centralized fossil fuel plants and concentrate more on rooftop solar and storage. Rive said Nevada is at an inflection point, and asked “Do we continue building old technology, or embrace the new energy economy?”

SolarCity has not installed or sold new rooftop solar systems in Nevada after the regulators adopted a new rate classification for net metering. Under this, homeowners get credit for the excess energy their systems generate. The solar firm said that 550 jobs were lost in Nevada because the new rates no longer provided any incentive to install rooftop solar systems.

In the comments provided to the Review-Journal, Rive said, “Tonight we presented to a group of Nevada’s leaders how innovative new technologies will enable a distributed energy grid that empowers customers,” and “We thank Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) for providing their Gigafactory to show this future of energy being built right here in Nevada.”
