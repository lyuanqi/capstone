Tesla , Inc (TSLA) Rolls Out New Linux Kernel Update

Published By News Desk at July 1, 2017 07:02 am The update has small software improvements only as it’s a backend update

Tesla Inc. (NASDAQ:TSLA) provides its customers with over-the-air software update, through which it makes changes to its current software, further improving it. One such improvement that the company has been touting for months, was the Linux Kernel update which had been delayed in the past. But not anymore, as the carmaker started pushing out the update this week.

According to Electrek, Linux Kernel update to the center console of vehicles was pushed out this week, but it was only a backend upgrade. The update was pushed out to the center screen of the vehicles only, while the update for the instrument cluster is still pending and may be rolled out soon. The latest software update of 8.1 has upgraded the Linux Kernel version from 2.6.36 to 4.4.35.

Through the new update, owners will witness minor changes in their software, while some other enhancements have also been made. But the new update was a blessing for the Tesla owners in Taiwan, as they received the Autopilot and navigation system in their EVs. The energy storage company started deliveries of its cars in Taiwan this year, and these features were not available at the time of delivery.

CEO Elon Musk has said that they would make significant changes to the user interface of the vehicle. With the Linux Kernel update already available, we can expect UI changes to come soon too. The update will also feature changes to the web browser used by only 30% of the owners, while media app will also be transformed.

Tesla has been putting in efforts to improve single features of its software, and recently, it was said that the car company is in talks with music labels to launch its music streaming service for its vehicles.
