    Here's Why Tesla (TSLA) Stock is Up Today

    Madeleine Johnson
    ZacksJune 20, 2016

    On Monday, shares of high-end electric car manufacturer Tesla Motors Inc. TSLA are up around 2% in late-afternoon trading after Panasonic, a prominent Japanese electronics company, announced they will be jumpstarting electric vehicle battery production at Teslas Gigafactory in Nevada.

    Actual battery cell production is likely to begin in November.

    The giant plant is now set to open its doors in July of this year, months ahead of its original schedule of early 2017. According to Nikkei Asian Review, Tesla is thought wo have called on Panasonic to compress its timeline after receiving a large number of preorders for its [lower-cost] Model 3 sedan, due out next year.

    Earlier this year, it was revealed that Panasonic agreed to invest up to $1.6 billion in eight installments in the Gigafactory, but this amount could potentially increase because the company would like to avoid any delays in the production of Teslas electric cars, an executive at Panasonic told the Nikkei Asian Review.

    Currently, Tesla sits at a #4 (Sell) on the Zacks Rank.
