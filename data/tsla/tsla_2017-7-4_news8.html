Tesla Inc and 3 More Inspiring American Business Stories

July 4, 2017 By Paul Shea Leave a Comment Filed Under: News, Stocks

It’s the Fourth of July, America’s Independence Day. As you join your family and friends to watch fireworks and celebrate the USA, think about some of the things that make it truly great. America is about freedom, refusing to bow down to authority, and the free expression of ideas. These are among the values that should ring out as we celebrate today.

But are these values still alive in America today? Politically, the country seems to have becomemore divided than ever but while America sorts out it’s issues,innovation is still strong in the United States. Let’s take a look at four US companies that are truly in line with the American Ideal and who are making waves in 2017.

Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (NASDAQ:AMD)

Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (NASDAQ:AMD) is not a new company by any standards. It has been based in California for the last 50 years. It’s one of the oldest chip makers in the world, and it’s a well known part of the tech landscape.

Why then is it important to be proud of Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (NASDAQ:AMD) in 2017? Because this is the year the firm became the sort of trust-buster that President Teddy Roosevelt could be proud of.

With the release of a multitude of new chip designs Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (NASDAQ:AMD) finally broke the Intel Corporation (NASDAQ:INTC) hold on the chip market this year.

The reason that AMD has managed to pull Intel back from complete market dominance is product quality. After years in the wilderness the firm has finally released a chip that buyers think matches up to Intel’s offering. It’s an incredible testament to the wealth of ability and determination in America’s companies, even those which look to be depressed for some time.

Farmer’s Business Network

You can’t buy stock in this firm, at least not yet, but it brings together multiple strands of what it means to be American. It’s a market information platform for farmers that allows them to see the prices of various commodities and make decisions on planting from home.

America was built on farming. For good and for bad, the country still has a firmly agrarian basis, and many of the states in the Union still rely on farming to keep them alive. America’s first great export was corn from the Mid West.

The opening up of the Eerie canal connected the great lakes region, and much of the Mid West to markets in Europe and across the world. The creation of a farmer information system finally allows those who make the food we eat able to plan easily and inexpensively from home.

The system shares and anonymizes all sorts of business data for farmers, giving them access to incredibly complex tools that they can use to become better at their work, and keep great America’s food tradition.

Tesla Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA)

There’s certainly a conversation to be had about how Tesla Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) gets subsidized by the tax payer, but that’s not what we’re celebrating this July 4th. Elon Musk founded the first real new American car company for decades, and it looks like he’s headed for success.

Through grit, determination, and a willingness to change the world, Mr. Musk is on the cusp of achieving something incredible. His firm is just about to launch a mass-market electric car. It’s going to be build in America using American parts and American labor, and it’s going to revolutionize the car industry.

If the tree of liberty must be refreshed with the blood of tyrants, then the tree of industry must be refreshed with the blood of stodgy, broken oligopolisers. America’s car country has been ruled for too long from Detroit, and those firm’s have not given customers what they want.

That has lead to the massive growth in imported cars, not because car buyers aren’t patriotic, but because for years Toyota, Honda and Audi produced much better products than any American company. Tesla Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) is going to change all that for the better.

The Model 3, which will be mass produced before the end of the year, is going to wake up America’s slumbering car-makers. That’s something that should make any patriot proud on July 4th.

Boston Beer Company Inc (NYSE:SAM)

The lesson learned on America’s frontier wasn’t that of the Westerns of the 1970s. People didn’t survive and thrive in America’s west by being the grittiest, most silent, most mysterious violent thug. They survived by being part of a community. No American company better enshrines the idea of community today than Boston Beer Company Inc (NYSE:SAM).

The Sam Adams brewer has long been fighting a heated war with the macro-breweries, and in 2017 you can see it pay off in an incredible way. You may not be sitting down to a Sam Adams to celebrate independence day, but it’s likely you’ll be drinking something that the Boston Beer Company Inc (NYSE:SAM) had a hand in making.

That’s because Boston Beer Company Inc (NYSE:SAM) sees itself, along with other smaller breweries, out on the frontier fighting the savage wilderness of corporate America. The firm is famed for helping out its smaller competitors.

Jim Koch, the firm’s founder, explains his philophy using biological rather than historical metaphor The reason Isupport competitorsbecomes obvious if you think about the way yeast ferments beer. If enough yeast are working together, they can change theecosystemfor the mutual benefit of all. If they aren’t, other organisms take over, and the yeast will fail. Craft brewing is kind of like that.”

Yeast is a little thematically off base for the fourth of July. As you knock back a few cold ones, it might be better to think of Sam Adams and the Boston Beer Company Inc (NYSE:SAM) as pioneers struggling to stake out a claim. They’re using the power of community to get there, just like the original pioneers did, and that’s something to be truly proud of as an American.
