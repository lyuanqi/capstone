Tesla Motors Not Able to Replicate US Sales in Europe

Published By News Desk at December 25, 2016 06:18 am The number of Model S sold during the year 2016 is lower than last year

While Tesla Motors Inc. (NASDAQ:TSLA) was able to gain a significant share of the automotive market in the United States and Canada, the company has not been able to replicate its success in the European region. The sales of Tesla cars have not been as strong as they were expected to be in Europe and SeekingAlpha analyst believes that it may be due to the lack of marketing or presence of better alternatives.  

Analyst Bill Maurer noted that the number of cars sold by Tesla has remained below the 2015 numbers since the month of June. The Y-T-D sales in July were below the 7,000 mark, and the company was not even able to breach the 10,000 units sold mark by the end of October, which, according to the data posted by the analyst, was one of the strongest months in 2016 for Tesla. Going into the month of November, the sales slowed down dramatically and was by far one of the worst months for the company.  

During the month of December this year, only 630 units of Model S were delivered to customers, which is a sharp decline from 1,600 units of Model S delivered last year. To summarize, Tesla was unable to maintain the level of sales it had in 2015 and the sold 2,700 fewer units of Model S and about 2,600 fewer units of Model X during the running year to date.  

The analyst believes that Tesla needs to improve its presence in Europe, notably Denmark, where the total sales of Model S thus far have only been 58 units.  

The analyst ratings for the company are one Buy, four Outperform, ten Hold, five Underperform, and one Sell. The closing price of the stock was $213.34 after gaining 2.35%.
