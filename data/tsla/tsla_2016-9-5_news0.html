Elon Musk Devoting Full-time to Ensure Tesla Motors Inc (TSLA) Model 3 Launches on Time!

Published By News Desk at September 5, 2016 09:22 am What’s more important in our view is a tight control on the expenses as the production starts

It seems that billionaire Elon Musk has finally realized the dangers surrounding EV Maker Tesla Motors Inc. (NASDAQ:TSLA), as he is now more than serious about fixing things up. In a recent address to his employees, the billionaire motivated them while saying that Q3 would be decisive for the twelve-year-old company’s future, as it would be closely watched by investors and other stakeholders. Moreover, he expressed his vision to change the image of the company from being a “cash burning entity” to a “profitable venture.”

That being said, Mr. Musk feels that it is high-time for the partners of his supply chain to realize the need of the hour. In response, to the billionaire’s urge of ensuring a timely launch for the Model 3, suppliers have shown their intent to make sure that things get done on time. Fanuc, a renowned name in industrial robots, is working with the EV maker to supply the robots for the production of the Model 3. The company spokesman said that they will give it their best shot in trying to ensure a timely launch, which seems to be a challenging yet attainable task.

The excitement of the 42-year-old CEO can be seen from the fact that he is now intolerant to all those who do not complete their task on time. In a recent statement, he said that those who do not get done on time would be cut out of the picture. One of the more important things in this regard is to ensure that there are no lacuna’s that persisted at the launch of Model X. That being said, he considers a timely launch is the need of the hour.

However, what’s more important in our view is a tight control on the expenses as the production starts. Tesla has struggled to post profits since its inception, and this may have dissuaded investors. Perhaps he should no doubt ensure a timely launch, but should also keep a tight check on the expenses, by making certain KPIs and standards.
