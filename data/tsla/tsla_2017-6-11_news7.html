Why investors should jump on Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) stock after a 6.5% crash last Friday.

Eddy Parton - June 11, 2017

Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) dominates the graphic chips industry, used in the gaming market. The company is doing very well in the data center market as well. One of the big reasons that NVDA stock has risen 350% since the start of 2016 is because of its performance in these two industries. As seen in the following chart from HadePlatform.com,Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA)data centers and gaming has quickly become anoverwhelming chunk of the company’s revenue pie. Collectively, the two businesses now account for about 75% ofNvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) total revenue, up from 45% in the same period two years ago.

Source: HadePlatform.com

With that said, Wall Street stopped seeing Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) as a graphic chip company, and realized its innovation and upside in other segments. This realization was driven by the fact thatNvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) capitalized on gaming and data center in such a dominant fashion. And has given Wall Street and NVDA stock owners real reason to believe thatNvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) will soon do the same inautonomous driving and AI.

Investors seekinga growth company that’s leveraged to the artificial intelligence and autonomous driving boom should consider Nvidia stock, especially with a 6.5% loss last Friday.

Products Made By Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA)

Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) Corporation designs, develops, and markets 3D graphic processors, chip sets and related multimedia software. A US company, Nvidia was founded in 1993 and headquartered in Santa Clara (Silicon Valley), California.

  • Graphics Processing Unit ((GPU)) (for desktop and notebook PCs) – GeForce discrete and chipset products. This is Nvidias main revenue source.
  • Tegra Processor (for tablets, smart phones and gaming devices) Processors that deliver superior visual and multimedia experience while consuming minimal power. An example is the Jetson TX2.
  • Tesla GPU (data centers) Used in supercomputers for high speed data analysis.

Bloomberg summarizes well; Nvidia has become one of the chipmakers leading the charge to provide the underpinnings of machine intelligence in everything from data centers to automobiles. As the biggest maker of graphics chips, Nvidia has proved that type of processors ability to perform multiple tasks in parallel has value in new markets, where artificial intelligence is increasingly important.

Below is a chart ofNvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) total revenue growth over the last few years, which is likely to accelerate.

Revenue drivers

Gaming (especially on PCs, desktop) Is the core of Nvidias revenues (see graph below), and obviously still a very important market. Nvidia dominates the GPU market with 80.3% revenue share in discreet graphics, followed by Advanced Micro Devices (NASDAQ:AMD), and Intel (NASDAQ:INTC). Nvidia has been successfully expanding into eSports games to broaden their base. More than 60% of Nvidias revenue comes from gaming.

Data Centers This has been a booming growth area recently for Nvidia, especially their Pascal-based Tesla GPUs, which are designed to support AI (artificial intelligence).

Automotive Tesla (NASDAQ:TSLA) have switched to using Nvidias chips for autonomous driving. Several other car companies also use or will soon be using Nvidia chips including Toyota, Audi, Mercedes, Honda, BMW, and Volvo. Even if we dont go to full level autonomous (level 5), Nvidia will have a market to make vehicles safer. This is a huge potential growth area.

Professional Visualization Quadro GPUs help engineers, designers, developers, and artists use emerging design techniques such as deep learning and fluid simulation to achieve high-end professional graphical rendering.

Nvidias customers

Nvidia has a very long list of customers as their graphic chips are the best. Some include Amazon (NASDAQ:AMZN), Google (NASDAQ:GOOG, GOOGL), IBM (NASDAQ: IBM), Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) (Xbox), Baidu (NASDAQ: BIDU), and Tencent (OTC:TCEHY).

Valuation

Nvidias currently has a market cap of US $84B, and enterprise value of US $79B.

Nvidia is a cash making machine with growing revenues, profit margins and profits. At the end of January 2017, Nvidia had a net cash position of US $4.8b. A shareholder bonus is Nvidias plan to return US $1Bin 2017 and US $1.3B in 2018 to shareholders. What might be more remarkable thanNvidia Corporation (NASDAQ:NVDA) revenue growth is its gross profit growth, which is actually growing faster than the former.

Current net profit margin is 24.11%, and 2018 PE is forecast at 45.9.

Analyst consensus target is US $125.59, with an outperform rating. A bit unusual given the target is 11% below the stock price. Probably due to the potential for continued high growth of revenues, diversifying into new growth areas.

Catalysts

  • According to a May 27, 2017 Bloomberg report, SoftBank (OTC:SFBTF) considers raising stake in U.S. Chipmaker Nvidia. Softbank already owns just under 5% of NVDA stock
  • Q3, 2017 and early 2018 The release of Nvidias new next generation Volta GPU (GTX 20 series).
  • Further automotive and medical diagnosis adoption of Nvidias chips.

Risks

  • Strong competition from AMD, Intel, Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL), and Qualcomm (NASDAQ:QCOM). Nvidias revenue market share of discrete graphics slipped by around 4% in Q1, 2017.
  • A slowdown in gaming sales, or a slowdown in the PC/desktop market. Q1, 2017 saw GPU shipments fall 16% quarter-over-quarter, more than the three-year average seasonal fall of 6%, due to a slowdown in gaming sales, after a strong December quarter with Christmas sales.
  • AI may not take off as is currently expected.
  • Nvidia carries a high earning multiple.
