Is The New Tesla Motors Battery Upgrade A Good Option For P90D Owners?

Published By News Desk at January 21, 2017 09:05 am Tesla updated its Design Studio with new battery pack options that boost the all-electric drive range on its vehicles impressively. We analyze if the upgrade is worth for current P90D owners

Tesla Motors Inc (NASDAQ:TSLA) last night brought in some solid developments for Tesla fans, as it updated its Design Studio with newer battery options for the hit-selling Model S and the Model X vehicles. A new 100 kWh battery pack as an option to each of the vehicles have pushed their ranges even further.

The Model S 90D which was previously capped at 294 miles can now drive for 335 miles on a single charge. The Model X 90D also gets a significant boost in the form of 40 miles of additional range, which now extends to just under 300 miles on a full charge.

Notably, however, while the range bump on the 100D option is only minimal compared to the P100D (which is the company’s performance vehicles with the upgraded 100 kWh battery pack) the price difference is comparatively much more significant. While the Model S P100D starts at $137,800 the non-performance 100D starts at $95,800. That translates to a price differential of $42,000 for just the added acceleration, the Smart Air Suspension feature and in fact, reduced range. The Model S 100D gives an additional 20 miles of range compared to the P100D version.

The same price differentials also apply to the Model X. The new P100D costs an additional $37,000 compared to the non-performance 100D version despite giving a marginal six miles of added electric range.

Quite clearly, the price differentials do not quite translate into the difference in terms of value at least for people who are currently not driving the performance versions of Tesla cars.

Still, the company’s move to boost the range of the Model S to an impressive 335 miles and the Model X just under 300 miles, with the 100 kWh battery packs is a smart move in a market said to have capped sales because of low-range electric vehicle options from other manufacturers.
