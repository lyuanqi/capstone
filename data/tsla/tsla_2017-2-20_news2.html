Taking a Look at the Data Behind Tesla, Inc. (TSLA)

By
Nick Cummings
-
February 20, 2017
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter
  • tweet

Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) is an interesting player in the Consumer Goods space, with a focus on Auto Manufacturers – Major. The stock has been active on the tape, currently trading at $272.23, up from yesterday’s close by 1.22%. Given the stock’s recent action, it seemed like a good time to take a closer look at the company’s recent data.

Fundamental Analysis

It’s important to get a feel for how any stock is actually valued on the market based on its core numbers. Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) currently trades with a market capitalization of $43.33 Billion. That value represents a market adjusting for revenues that have been growing by 145.35 % on a quarterly year/year basis as of the company’s last quarterly report.



The balance sheet health of any company plays a key role in its ability to meet its obligations and maintain the faith of its investment base. For TSLA, the company currently has $3.11 Billion of cash on the books, which is offset by $263.37 Million current liabilities. You can get a sense of how sustainable that is by a levered free cash flow of $-875.87 Million over the past twelve months. Generally speaking, earnings are expected to grow in coming quarters. Analysts are forecasting earnings of $-1.19 on a per share basis this quarter. Perhaps, that suggests something about why 0.40% of the outstanding share supply is held by institutional investors.

Technical Analysis

It’s important to check the technicals to get a sense of how TSLA has been acting. Looking at the stock’s movement on the chart, Tesla, Inc. recorded a 52-week high of $287.39. It is now trading 15.16% off that level. The stock is trading $234.25 its 50-day moving average by -37.98%. The stock carved out a 52-week low down at $156.68.

In recent action, Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) has made a move of +11.24% over the past month, which has come on Strong relative transaction volume. Over the trailing year, the stock is underperforming the S&P 500 by 20.65, and it’s gotten there by action that has been more volatile on a day-to-day basis than most other stocks on the exchange. In terms of the mechanics underlying that movement, traders will want to note that the stock is trading on a float of 27.10% with $126.75 Million sitting short, betting on future declines. That suggests something of the likelihood of a short squeeze in shares of TSLA.
