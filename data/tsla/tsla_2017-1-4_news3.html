    GM shares jump on sales climb; Tesla deliveries fall short; Oppenheimer says buy Panera Bread

    Seana Smith
    Yahoo FinanceJanuary 4, 2017

    Yahoo Finance is tracking the stocks you’re following, based on your Yahoo Finance ticker searches.

    General Motors (GM) – The US auto giant posted a 10% jump in December US sales, announcing that it sold 319,108 vehicles. Kelley Blue Book estimated GM would report a 4% climb.

    Tesla (TSLA) – The electric car maker delivered about 22,000 cars during the fourth quarter of 2016, bringing its total number shipped in 2016 to 76,230 cars. This fell short of Tesla’s full-year forecast of 80,000 to 90,000.

    Panera Bread (PNRA) – Oppenheimer upgraded the restaurant chain to outperform from perform and raised its price target to $240. Analyst Brian Bittner notes that he thinks Panera’s expanding delivery service will continue to boost its business.

    Amazon (AMZN) – The online retail giant reported that it had shipped 50% more items this holiday season compared to last year’s for third-party vendors and doubled its total for 2016. Amazon also announced plans to open a second fulfillment center in Jacksonville, Florida, adding more than 1,000 full-time employees.

    Ford (F) – The US automaker’s F-Series posted its best monthly sales in 11 years in December. Ford ended the year with total US sales of 2,614,697 vehicles, marking its best performance in 10 years.

    Delta Air Lines (DAL) – The airliner posted an increase in traffic in December. Delta reported revenue passenger miles rose 2.6% on a capacity increase of 1.5%.

    Alibaba (BABA) – The Chinese e-commerce giant is suing two vendors for selling fake Swarovski watches on its platform. This is Alibaba’s first legal action against counterfeiters in China.

    Amgen (AMGN) – Sanofi (SNY) and Regeneron Pharmaceuticals (REGN) have lost their bid to overturn a court verdict of two patents for Amgen’s cholesterol drug Repatha. Sanofi and Regeneron Pharmaceuticals make a rival drug.
