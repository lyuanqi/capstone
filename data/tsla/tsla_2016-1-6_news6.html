Will Faraday Future Pose a Challenge for Tesla (TSLA)?

January 06, 2016, 05:00:00 PM EDT By Zacks Equity Research, Zacks.com

Shutterstock photo

Tesla Motors, Inc. TSLA , which occupies a dominant position in the luxury electric vehicle market, may soon face competition from Faraday Future, a startup backed by a Chinese billionaire investor. On Monday, Faraday showcased an outstanding electric-powered concept vehicle, the FFZero1, at the Consumer Electronics Show (CES) 2016. It is the first concept car from Faraday.

Faraday, based in Gardena, CA, will be making an investment of $1 billion for a manufacturing plant in North Las Vegas, NV, over the coming weeks. The plant will create 4,500 new jobs. With this, Faraday expects to start production of its first electric vehicle in a couple of years. The company is entering the electric vehicle market in order to meet the changing demand in the industry.

The FFZero1 concept vehicle is based on Faraday's modular engineering platform, Variable Platform Architecture ("VPA"), in which the battery structure is arranged into strings. Altering the strings will change the battery capacity. The concept vehicle is a single seater and has four quad core motors with over 1,000 horsepower. This enables the vehicle to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in less than 3 seconds. The concept vehicle has a maximum speed of over 200 mph.

Faraday claims to be a fast moving company. Founded just 18 months ago, the company is already gearing up for a 3 million square feet manufacturing plant in Nevada and aims to deliver its first electric vehicle in the next couple of years.

The production vehicle, which will be launched in a couple of years based on the FFZero1 concept vehicle, will definitely stir competition in the market. However, Tesla, which was founded in 2003, has reached its position over the years. Further, by the time Faraday actually launches a production vehicle, Tesla is expected to announce new vehicles and technologies. In order to provide tough competition to Tesla, Faraday needs to work efficiently and at a rapid pace.

Faraday stated that it will be focusing on customers of luxury gasoline-powered vehicles to expand in the small electric vehicle market in the U.S. In the growing market for non-gasoline vehicles, opportunity exists for both the companies to prosper.

Tesla currently carries a Zacks Rank #4 (Sell).

Better-ranked automobile stocks include Lear Corp. LEA , Autoliv, Inc. ALV and O'Reilly Automotive Inc. ORLY , all carrying a Zacks Rank #2 (Buy).
