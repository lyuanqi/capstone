Watch for these posh stocks to pop higher if Brexit wins

By Victor Reklaitis

Published: June 23, 2016 8:42 a.m. ET

Share

Critical information before the U.S. market’s open

By

VictorReklaitis

Markets writer

It’s decision day in the U.K., and rain-splashed voters have been hitting the polling stations to choose whether to Brexit or Bremain.

Mr. Market’s main preoccupation lately has been Britain’s historic vote on European Union membership, but the referendum isn’t the only show in town.

The Fed, for example, is set to reveal after the close how banks performed in annual stress tests. You also could start worrying about Spain’s election this weekend.

And a raft of U.S. economic data today will help traders pass the time as they wait for the Brexit results.

Reports from the first counting areas are expected around 7:30 p.m. Eastern, with all in/out votes likely tallied by 2 a.m. Eastern.

The “remain” camp generally has looked like it will win, especially if you go by what the bookies say. A fresh Ipsos Mori survey out the day of voting is bearing that out, with a four-point lead for the anti-Brexit camp.

The U.K.’s FTSE 100 has put itself “within touching distance of its June high, a clear sign that investors at least are now relatively confident that the U.K. will vote to remain in the EU,” writes IG’s Chris Beauchamp.

But the “leave” side has been ahead in some recent polls. Stocks worldwide, and other assets perceived as risky, are expected to drop if the outcome goes that way.

Yet a Brexit wouldn’t be bad for all stocks, according to our call of the day. Certain luxury stocks will jump, say Exane BNP Paribas analysts. More on that below.

Meanwhile, today’s chart suggests bitcoin has been hurt by the “remain” camp finding some traction.

Key market gauges

S&P US:ESU6  and Dow futures US:YMU6 are pointing higher. Europe SXXP, -1.02%  stocks are gaining, with the U.K.’s FTSE 100 UKX, -0.47%  up after already rising for four sessions in a row.

The pound GBPUSD, +0.1850%   jumped to its highest level this year, above $1.49, after the Ipsos Mori poll Thursday showed “remain” in the lead.

Asian markets closed mixed. Oil US:CLQ6  is advancing, while gold US:GCQ6  and a key dollar index DXY, -0.29% were both lower.

The call

A Brexit should send the pound GBPUSD, +0.1850%  and euro EURUSD, +0.2923%  falling against the dollar, and U.K. and European luxury stocks should benefit from these “FX tailwinds,” say Exane BNP Paribas analysts in note.

“All else being equal, this should push up U.K. and EU luxury share prices in the short term,” they write.

The idea is these companies spend significantly in the U.K. and Europe to produce their goods, but then they sell to people all over, especially to Asians and Americans. Those sales could be supported by a softer pound and euro.

The analysts name names. In the event of a Brexit, they’re bullish in the near term on LVMH Moet Hennessy Louis Vuitton LVMHF, -0.99% MC, -1.37%  , Burberry BURBY, -0.73% BRBY, -0.73%  , Kering PPRUY, -1.06% KER, -1.54%  and Hermes HESAY, -0.55% RMS, -0.94%  .

They’re similarly upbeat on stocks such as Jimmy Choo JYMHF, -1.15% CHOO, -1.79%  , Prada PRDSY, +0.98% 1913, +1.79%  , Salvatore Ferragamo SFRGY, +4.87% SFER, -1.10% , Luxottica US:LUX LUX, -1.83%  and Moncler MONRF, -2.32% MONC, -0.84%   .

But looking beyond the short term and outside the U.K. and Europe, the analysts offer a warning on the broad sector. “Overall, adverse global GDP impacts and higher uncertainty connected to Brexit should be negative for luxury goods,” the Exane note says.

The chart

Remember bitcoin? Everyone’s favorite cryptocurrency has been pulling back as a Brexit has looked less likely, notes the Daily Shot newsletter.

“Bitcoin enthusiasts continue to insist that these two charts (below) are unrelated — Bitcoin’s sharp decline is all just due to Bitfinex exchange outage, etc. Right,” the newsletter says.

The quote

“Markets don’t appear to be putting too much stock in the ‘referendum weather theory.’ The rainy forecast for Thursday theoretically favors ‘leave.’ Leave voters are thought to be more determined, so more likely to brave bad weather.” — Jasper Lawler at CMC Markets ponders Brexit and the U.K.’s wet weather in a note.

Read more: Brexit day’s wet weather: It could be raining on the “remain” parade

The buzz

Are the unicorns bouncing back? Cloud communications company Twilio TWLO, -0.42%   priced its IPO above the expected range.

Red Hat RHT, +0.02%  is down premarket after falling late Wednesday as the software company announced it will buy 3scale, which specializes in managing APIs. Red Hat also posted quarterly profit that matched forecasts.

Bed Bath & Beyond BBBY, -0.95% is losing ground as well, after its quarterly results.

Tesla TSLA, -0.46%  is in the red premarket as investors continue to react to its explanation for its surprise SolarCity US:SCTY  bid.

The weak Canadian dollar USDCAD, -0.4130%   is getting blamed for the NHL picking Vegas over Quebec.

The two founders of a U.K. hedge fund are even split over Brexit, Financial News notes.

The economy

A report on U.S. weekly jobless claims came out ahead of the open, showing fewer claims than expected.

Watch for a manufacturing figure at 9:45 a.m., then two more economic releases at 10 a.m. — leading indicators and new-home sales.

Dallas Fed President Rob Kaplan is due to speak in New York at 7 p.m. Eastern.
