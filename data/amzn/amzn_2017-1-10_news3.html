    We continue to believe that Netflix is overvalued.

    Netflix (ticker: NFLX) shares may trade lower on first-quarter guidance. We expect first-quarter guidance for subscriber net additions of 1.50 million domestic and 3.75 million international, down from 2.23 million and 4.51 million, respectively, last year.     Between the two, Amazon is now the lower-priced option, while Netflix currently has greater catalog depth. We expect only modest first-quarter earnings-per-share growth on substantial revenue growth, reflecting the lack of leverage in Netflix’s model as content spending soaks up revenue growth.

    We are maintaining our Underperform rating and 12-month price target of $60.

    We believe that Netflix’s current valuation is unwarranted given the potential for sustained decelerating domestic growth coupled with consistently elusive international profitability, in addition to increased competition.

    Netflix continues to burn cash to fund its acquisition of original and exclusive content, while international profitability remains elusive and competition for both content and subscribers is becoming more fierce. Cash burn is accelerating, and we remain skeptical that the company’s content library justifies its high level of spending.

    We think that Netflix’s current share price reflects limited competition from Amazon, which recently expanded internationally; in order to compete, Netflix must either spend more or see its international subscriber growth slow. While we expect higher spending, Netflix’s international growth could slow by roughly two million in 2017.

    Netflix will report fourth-quarter results after the market closes on Wednesday, Jan. 18.

    Fourth-quarter results should meet or exceed subscriber guidance. We expect revenue of $2.48 billion and EPS of 13 cents versus consensus estimates for revenue of $2.47 billion and EPS of 13 cents, and guidance for EPS of 13 cents. Net subscriber additions should be in line with or above guidance of 1.45 million domestic and 3.75 million international, compared to net additions of 1.56 million domestic and 4.04 million internationally in fourth-quarter 2015. Guidance should be attainable given the positive reception to The Crown in the fourth quarter (Golden Globe recipient for best TV Drama), likely offsetting some churn from the last of the price increases. Additionally, Amazon did not compete with Netflix on price in the fourth quarter as we had expected it to do by marketing its monthly Prime Video ($8.99) offering. It is likely that Netflix will deliver significant EPS upside, as recent price increases are likely to drop to the bottom line.
