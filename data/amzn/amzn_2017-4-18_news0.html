Does Amazon Stock Have More Near Term Upside?

Posted by Vikram Nagarkar on April 18, 2017, 06:30 AM EST
Related Ticker: AMZN

AMZN stock is now up by over 20% in the year to date. Do Amazon shares have near term upside left?


Flickr

Shares of Seattle, Washington-based Amazon (NASDAQ:AMZN) have run up nicely this year, gaining by over 20% in the Year To Date (YTD). That compares with a 8.8% gain in the NASDAQ, in 2017 so far. Have Amazon shares got more upside left in the near term? As things stand, they probably do. While AMZN stock continues to get upgrades and target price hikes from analysts, the stock's technicals also look supportive of a further uptick from the current stock price.

Another Analyst Joins The $1000 Price Target Club.

Stephen Ju of Credit Suisse joined the group of analysts who have a $1000+ price target on AMZN stock. Ju put his weight behind Amazon stock, assigning it with a $1050 price target. The analyst hiked his price target by a good 16.6% from his previous price target of $900 a share, maintaining his outperform rating on the stock. Interestingly, Ju hiked his price target on AMZN stock even as he cut his Earnings Per Share (EPS) forecasts for FY 2017, FY 2018 and FY 2019. Quoting the source, StreetInsider:

"Analyst Stephen Ju raises his PT ahead of Amazon's Q1 report and continues to believes the company will re-establish its eCommerce segment operating margins expansion as AMZN grows its infrastructure further. He also sees margins benefiting as shipping losses moderate and sees upside to AWS revenue forecasts."

The Credit Suisse analyst is not alone. several analysts have been weighing in on Amazon with price targets in excess of $1000 a share. More recently, Needham & Co.’s Kerry Rice, upgraded the stock from 'hold' to 'buy', citing "several multibillion TAM [total addressable market] opportunities." Whether or not you look at Amazon's prospects in other businesses, there's a strong case for Amazon in e-commerce alone.

As we'd recently highlighted, online retail sales figures accounted for just 8.7% of total retail sales in 2016. That's expected to touch 10% in 2017. Moreover, the value of global online retail sales is expected to double over 2016 numbers, to $4 trillion by 2020. Yet, online retail sales will still account for just 14.6% of total retail sales, highlighting the true untapped potential over the coming decade. To further its cause, Amazon is also making strategic acquisitions, like that of Souq.com, which will help the e-commerce giant expand its global footprint.

In markets like India as well, Amazon's aggressive expansion has forced competitors to explore M&A activities to consolidate their competitive abilities. Then there's the tailwind from Amazon becoming the preferred search option for online buyers, which could potentially take revenue away from the likes of Alphabet Inc (NASDAQ:GOOGL), more so with voice based search expected to close the gap with traditional typing-based search. That's a trend Amazon is gearing up to exploit, with its Alexa. And of course, there are those other business segments apart from e-commerce, like AWS, among several others, like live video streaming, which Amazon seems to be taking rather seriously.

AMZN Stock Technical Charts Still Look Supportive Of An Uptick.

Possibly the only bearish signal from Amazon's technical charts is the one from the Relative Strength Index (RSI). The RSI suggests that the stock is overbought at this point, with the reading above 70, the most commonly used threshold to signal an overbought condition. However, the RSI is usually used in tandem with the Bollinger Bands, which at this point, don't concur with the RSI. Going by the Bollinger Bands, there's more room for upside.

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) has started closing the gap with the signal line, but it hasn't crossed over yet. Amazon's stock price is still above its 20 day and 50 day Simple Moving Averages (SMAs), and the recent correction in the stock from its 52 week high of ~$924 a share has come largely on the back of declining volumes, which suggests that the trend may not sustain. Based on a combination of fundamental and technical analysis, AMZN stock looks well poised to continue its run in the near term.
