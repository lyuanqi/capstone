Amazon Stock Could Fly Higher From Here. Here Is Why (NASDAQ:AMZN)

Posted by Virendra Singh Chauhan on May 02, 2017, 06:08 AM EST
Related Ticker: AMZN

Amazon.com Inc. beat analyst estimates on topline as well as bottom line. Is Amazon stock a good buy following the latest earnings report.


Flickr

Seattle, Washington-based online retailing giant Amazon (NASDAQ:AMZN) reported its Q1 2017 earnings last week, on April 27. And, investors have been cheering AMZN stock higher since then. AMZN stock price is up 3.25% in the two trading sessions following the earnings announcement, closing the last trading session at a price of $948.23 per share. Was the earnings report really as good as the stock price movement makes it look? AMZN stock is up by a solid 26.5% in the year-to-date. Can Amazon stock maintain the current momentum? Well, we think it can. And, here is why.

Amazon Q1 Earnings: A Beat Really?

A number of Amazon bulls have been cheering the latest numbers. Amazon reported EPS of $1.48 per share, a 35 cent surprise, on a revenue of $35.7 billion, which was $400M above estimates. While Amazon did beat analysts' topline/earnings estimates, a deeper look throws up some startling facts. As we had pointed out in our Amazon Q1 earnings preview, the analyst consensus had dropped from expectations for EPS of $1.7 on revenue of $35.7B just 3 months ago. And, the major cause of the reduction in the consensus estimates was the weaker than expected Q1 guidance Amazon had issued during its Q4 2016 earnings report. In other words, while Amazon did beat on the headline numbers, the beat was helped in a large part by its sandbagged guidance.

Also, the earnings beat came in spite of a 6% YoY decline in Operating Income. Without taking credit away from Amazon, the beat was largely attributable to a lower tax rate this quarter in comparison to Q1 2016. In other words, the entire EPS beat was a function of a lower tax rate rather than business outperformance as such. In fact, operating margins declined to 28.14%, a significant fall from 36.8% in Q1 2016, with the international retail segment being the major driver of the margin compression. Hence, the Q1 headline 'beat' isn't something we are too excited about. However, the Q1 report had a number of other positives which we are bullish about.

Amazon Prime Revenue Growth

Amazon reorganized its revenue reporting segments. The company, from Q1 2017, started reporting revenue from retail subscription services (Prime and other digital content subscription revenue). And the segment posted a solid 49.2% YoY growth in Q1 2017, coming in at a quarterly revenue of $1.94 billion. The strong growth in this segment is a proof of the momentum the firms 'Prime' membership program is enjoying. According to recent data from Central Intelligence Research Partners (CIRP), the 'Prime' program now has 80M subscribers, up 38% from last year.

The rapidly growing prime member base has huge implications not only for the subscription-based revenue but also for the retail revenues the company reports. Why? As we have highlighted multiple times in the past, a Prime Member outspends a non-prime counterpart by 2.4x, which implies that rapid growth of the prime member base will also lead to incremental growth for retail product revenue. Hence, the rapidly growing subscription revenue is a positive for Amazon and this is a metric we will closely watch over the coming quarters.

Amazon Ad Revenue On The Rise?

Amazon also reported an 'Other' category which includes Ad-related revenue and revenue from co-branded credit cards. A colleague had covered, earlier this year, how Amazon could be becoming a threat to online advertising giant Alphabet (NASDAQ:GOOGL). With over half the e-commerce now originating on Amazon search, Amazon seemed to be eating into Google's forte: Online search. The retail major now has a thriving advertising business which was reflected in the growth of the 'Other' segment. The segment saw revenue grow to $850M in Q1, up 56% YoY over Q1 2016. While the 'Other' segment seems to be meager in comparison to Amazon's overall numbers, it's a potentially new revenue stream for Amazon. And, given the huge data trove that Amazon is now generating/collecting, this segment could go onto become a huge part of the overall scheme of things.

FBN Securities analyst Shebly Seyrafi drew focus to this segment while hiking his Amazon stock price target to $1050 from $925, following the latest earnings release. As reported by investors.com, Shebly wrote: "There has been much discussion in the analyst community that Amazon's advertising revenue is growing rapidly and could be a threat to Alphabet longer-term."

Conclusion

Amazon apparently reported a solid beat on top line/earnings in its latest earnings report. However, the beat was achieved on significantly lowered estimates, and hence, that isn't something which would get us too excited in the name. Aside from the revenue/EPS beat, the company did have multiple bright spots with solid growth in its AWS segment, Subscription based revenues and Ad-related revenue. These are comparatively small revenue segments for the Seattle, Washington-based company and could become long-term growth drivers given the tailwinds in the respective markets (Cloud and ad markets expected to grow into future). The strength of these implications is also reflected in the increasing bullishness around AMZN stock among Wall Street analysts. The Wall Street consensus price target for Amazon stock has climbed up from $978, prior to the latest earnings call, to $1079 currently, implying a further 13.8% upside from the last closing price of AMZN stock. In short, Amazon continues to be a good long-term buy.
