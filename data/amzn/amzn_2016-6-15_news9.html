Bed Bath & Beyond Unfolds Acquisition to Stem Amazon's Advance

By

Anders Keitz

Follow
 | Jun 15, 2016 | 11:31 AM EDT

As many retailers fall to Amazon's (AMZN) pricing power, Bed Bath & Beyond (BBBY) took steps to defend itself by purchasing One Kings Lane, an online home goods retailer, in an all-cash deal late Tuesday, though the amount was undisclosed.

"This is a tremendous opportunity," One Kings Lane CEO Dinesh Lathi said in a statement, "to benefit from additional support and resources and gain exposure to new customers."

The deal will not affect Bed Bath & Beyond's fiscal first-quarter results, as the transaction occurred during the second quarter. The company expects the deal to weigh slightly on earnings for the full year. Shares were up shortly after the bell Wednesday, having closed the previous day down slightly at $42.81.

Bed Bath and Beyond CEO Steve Temares said One Kings Lane will serve as a cornerstone for the company's growing offerings in furniture and home decor, adding that together they will be able to do more for their customers "wherever, whenever and however" they wish to interact.

News of the acquisition comes after Amazon -- which is a holding in the Growth Seeker portfolio -- started asserting its dominance over the home goods bricks-and-mortar retailer.

According to a second-quarter survey by KeyBanc, analysts found significant product overlap with Amazon, up to 76% from 68% during the first quarter. Furthermore, the analysts found that Bed Bath & Beyond had an 8% price disadvantage vs. Amazon.

But this deal could put Bed Bath & Beyond back in control. TheStreet's Jim Cramer said on CNBC's Squawk on the Street Wednesday that the move by Bed Bath and Beyond is an internet strategy. "To say that they are finally doing something about the Web means that this stock has a chance to turn around," said Cramer, adding that it was smart to do something other than buying back stock.

"This great short that has been Bed Bath and Beyond may be over," said Cramer.

-- This story has been updated with additional quotes from Jim Cramer.
