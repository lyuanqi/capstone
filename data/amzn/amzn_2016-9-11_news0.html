Amazon Stock (AMZN) Joins in Global Rout

By
Sam Bourgi
-
September 11, 2016 - 21:00 UTC
Tweet
Share1
+12
StockTwits   
Share

Shares of Amazon, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMZN) plunged Friday, reversing gains for the week after Federal Reserve rate-hike fears attracted sellers to the market.

Amazon shares fell $23.92 or 3.1% to close at $760.14 on Friday, the lowest settlement since August 25. For the week, Amazon finished off 1.6%.

The global e-commerce giant fell with the rest of the market after Federal Reserve officials swung the door wide open on a rate increase. Fed Bank of Boston President Eric Rosengren was especially hawkish by stating he supported gradual rate increases. Rosengren, who is also a voting member of the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC), believes that by not raising interest rates soon, the Fed puts itself in the position of having to tighten policy much faster down the road.

Amazon shares were trading at all-time highs following the Labour Day weekend, having gained a staggering 50% year-over-year on improved growth prospects and the strong performance of its cloud computing platform. Year-to-date, Amazon is vastly outpacing the S&P 500’s information technology index as well as the Nasdaq.

The company’s strong performance reflects its ambitious business plan, which extends far beyond e-commerce or even cloud computing.

“We seek to be Earth’s most customer-centric company for four primary customer sets: consumers, sellers, enterprises, and content creators” Amazon says in its corporate Mission Statement.

What’s more, the e-commerce industry is said to be just getting started. Even in the United States, a country known for strong e-commerce trading, online retail sales account for just 8% of all sales, according to the Commerce Department. That says nothing of the massive international market, especially in emerging economies with a growing middle class.

Wall Street could experience renewed volatility this week, as investors tread cautiously after last week’s selloff. As a result, Amazon and other tech-related stocks are likely to be subject to the same pressures as the broader market.
