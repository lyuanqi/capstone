Oracle Will Lose Database Battle in Amazon’s Cloud, Says Global Equities

By
Tiernan Ray
Dec. 16, 2016 3:26 p.m. ET
  • Text Size
  • Regular
  • Medium
  • Large
    •

Although Oracle (ORCL) founder and chairman CTO Larry Ellison yesterday trumpeted the company’s progress in sales of its latest database version, Global Equities’s Trip Chowdhry today opines, citing what he says are numerous conferences he’s attended, that Oracle is going to lose the database wars in cloud computing against Amazon.com (AMZN).

Said Ellison yesterday,

Our Database as a Service cloud revenue was $100 million for the quarter, driving growth in our overall database business. We expect our Database as a Service and IaaS businesses will grow even faster than our skyrocketing SaaS business. A lot of people were taken by surprise when IDC ranked Oracle #1 in Enterprise SaaS, surpassing salesforce.com and overcoming their fifteen year head-start. Stay tuned. More surprises coming. I think we’re going to do even better with IaaS and the Oracle database in the cloud.

However, Chowdhry, referencing Amazon's recent “re:invent” conference for its AWS cloud computing service, writes, "we feel it is impossible for ORCL to increase its DataBase Revenues."

In face, "ORCL Database on AMZN-AWS will shrink, as this is the first step in migrating out of ORCL Database,” he opines.

This is how the customer is thinking about "move out of ORCL" Step -1: First Step - do the complete relocate the Database to AMZN-AWS - in EC2 instances from on-premises- this is Revenue neutral Step 2: Move some workloads from Step 1 using a portfolio of AMZN-AWS RDS Services - which initially is a combination of ORCL, MYSQL, DynamoDB, and Aurora - this is Revenue negative Step 3: Move some workloads from Step 1 and Step 2 to AWS Aurora and AWS DynamoDB - this is Revenue negative Further Challenge: Just a month back MSFT had launched its SQL Server for Linux...previously SQL Server only ran on Windows Server...the customer will not switch from ORCL to MSFT...but it gives another bargaining parameter to the customer We have attended infinite conferences and attended infinite sessions, we are not seeing any new workloads going into ORCL database We just don't see how ORCL database revenues could grow.

Oracle stock today is down $1.36, or 2%, at $66.67. Amazon shares are down $4.20, or 0.6%, at $756.80.
