Is CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) Stock a Good Investment?

By
Fahad Saleem, Contributing Editor
-
02/20/2017
0
792
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter
  • tweet

CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) earned $1.71 per share in the fourth quarter, better than the analysts’ forecast of $1.67, while revenue in the quarter totaled to $46 billion, versus the consensus estimate of $46.5 billion. For the full year of 2017, the company expects EPS of $5.77-$5.93, versus the consensus of $5.86. CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) has roughly 10,000 retail pharmacy stores.

CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) is one of the largest pharmacy retailers in the US. CVS Health (Caremark) PBM (Pharmacy Benefits Management) division is the company’s largest revenue generators. In 2016, pharmacy services sales totaled to about $120 billion, with revenue growth rate of 19.5% year over year. The company, however, had to cut its 2016 outlook and give 2017 estimates that were less than the Streets projections after Walgreens Boots Alliance got a contract from Prime Therapeutics last year. Prime Therapeutics is owned by 14 Blue Cross and Blue Shield health plans, which cover about 22 million Americans. CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) was also stunned after US military insurance program Tricare signed a deal with Walgreens in September 2016. The two deals collectively will take away 40 million retail prescriptions from CVS Health each year.
 
Company’s full-year guidance assumes the completion of $5.0 billion stock buyback program. The company will reduce the share count by 6% during the year. The company repurchased its stock in 2016 as well.
 
CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) stock has lost about 30% since May 2016. The merger of Walgreens Boots Alliance (NASDAQ:WBA) and Rite Aid Corp (NYSE:RAD) is one of the biggest threats to the company’s market share. If the merger goes through, CVS will have a behemoth competitor to deal with.
 
Investors are also skeptical about CVS Health Corp (NYSE:CVS) stock amid the new government’s plans to repeal Obamacare, which could result in lower demands for prescription drugs. The government is also planning a crackdown against sales of high-priced specialty drugs.
