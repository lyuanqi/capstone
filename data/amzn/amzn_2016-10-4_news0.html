Is Amazon.com (AMZN) the 'Must Own' Stock for the Next 5 Years? Top Analysts Respond

Tuesday's 'Fast Money Halftime Report' panel on CNBC reacted to Piper Jaffray research analyst Gene Munster's bullish call on Amazon.com (AMZN).

Giovanni Bruno
Oct 4, 2016 1:12 PM EDT

NEW YORK (TheStreet) --Piper Jaffray research analyst Gene Munster called Amazon.com (AMZN) the "best" stock for the next five years, believing the e-commerce giant will continue to gain online market share. Munster currently has an "overweight" rating on the stock with a $900 price target.

In response to Munster's call, Tuesday's "Fast Money Halftime Report" panel on CNBC discussed the potential for Amazon stock moving forward.

"I agree with the call I think Amazon is a fantastic company. When you look at it technically the chart looks really strong, pristine, and you really can't find a point of reference to get out until $700," Virtus Investment Partners chief market strategist Joe Terranova said.

He pointed to two catalysts for Amazon which he thinks makes it a must own.

"First of all, there is really is no competitor that you can clearly define. The second thing is when you're looking at designing your portfolio, Amazon fits in your consumer discretionary sleeve," Terranova added.

Lebenthal Asset Management CEO Jim Lebenthal noted that the stock is probably going to move higher towards the end of the year, however, he sees a reason to approach it cautiously.

"What you've got to think about if you're a value investor like me is the margin of safety. The stock trades around 75 times next year's earnings. It would not be crazy for it to trade at 60 times next year's earnings and that would be a 20% hit, that's why I don't own it," he explained.
