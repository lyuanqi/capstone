AMZN Stock: Why Amazon.com, Inc. Stock Is Still A Screaming Buy?

Posted by Sreekanth Anasa on March 31, 2017, 07:31 AM EST
Related Ticker: AMZN

Should you buy into Amazon.com, Inc. stock at these all-time highs? Here is why AMZN stock is still a screaming buy at its all-time highs.


Flickr

Shares of Seattle, Washington-based online retail giant Amazon.com, Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) are on a tear, gaining more than 47% in the last 12-months. AMZN stock closed at a new all-time high of $876.34 in yesterday's trading session, with an intra-day high of $877.06. Such was the effect of an uptick in AMZN share price yesterday that it was enough to make Amazon CEO, Jeff Bezos the second richest man in the world displacing Berkshire's (NYSE:BRK.B) Warren Buffet. Many might think that AMZN stock may not have much upside left after rising a massive 134% in the last two years. Think again, as we recently highlighted, Amazon stock could surge past the $1000 mark. Now the obvious question is 'Should you buy AMZN stock at its all-time highs?' Does Amazon.com, Inc (NASDAQ: AMZN) stock has much upside left? We believe that AMZN stock has tremendous upside left. AMZN stock is still a screaming buy. Here's why.

Barclays tips Amazon to be worth $910 billion in 10 years time.

Barclays analyst Ross Sandler had recently initiated coverage on AMZN stock with an Overweight rating and a $1,120 price target, implying more than 27% upside. Ross Sandler's 12-month bull case suggests more than 53% upside from yesterday's close with a price target of $1344. He further went on to say that the eCommerce behemoth could be worth $910 billion in 10 years time. Ross Sandler and his research team, in their research note, wrote "AMZN is likely to be one of the first trillion-dollar market cap companies; it’s just a question of when, not if, in our view, AMZN is arguably the best story in the space, with the most open-ended growth opportunity & most highly functional organization."

Sandler and his team backed their prediction with some key points. They expect Amazon Prime members to increase to 450 million members from the current 100 million in 10 years. Amazon retail will penetrate further deeper into new emerging markets such as South East Asia and South America and is projected to deliver revenues of around $520 billion from retail operations in the same time frame as above. As we have reported earlier, Amazon's push into untapped new markets with the acquisition of Souq.com will be a catalyst for AMZN stock, going ahead. Barclays research team hints that Amazon would become the go-to place for everything with more new services added in future and it would be building more on its present new initiatives like Prime video. They also see Alexa growing into every sphere of our life soon. We had also previously reported that Alexa could be a $10 billion revenue opportunity for Amazon in the days to come.

As the Barclays team claims, Amazon reaching one trillion market cap is just a matter of time, which would imply a stock price of almost $2000, indicating a humongous upside from its present stock price. Well, that's not an opportunity to forgo, and the Barclays prediction could, very well on the cards. An Economist special report states Amazon as the world's most remarkable firm and it suggests that the retail giant has just got started.

Public Cloud could become a utility like electricity, Amazon's AWS could gain big.

We all know that Amazon is continuing its remarkable run driven by the twin growth engines of eCommerce and cloud computing. Its most profitable growth engine story gets further encouragement from Morgan Stanley's latest study. A Geekwire post reports that Morgan Stanley's latest research suggests "public cloud has the potential to become a global utility, analogous to electricity." This would mean more extensive adoption of cloud by business customers, which is good news for Amazon since AWS is the market leader in cloud. Morgan Stanley analyst Katy Huberty and her team, in their report, predict overall market opportunity for the public cloud to grow to $340 billion by 2020. For comparison, the report states that the current electricity market is worth $1.6 trillion. The valuation of AWS could jump by 18% from Morgan Stanley's base case scenario, if their bull case turns out to be true. The Barclays research team also predicted a rosy future for AWS with it growing into "a massive global enterprise with tens of millions of paying customers and $76 billion in annual revenue."

To quote analyst Katy Huberty from the report: "If public cloud is a transformational technology like electricity, usage broadens and workload growth accelerates (our bull case), implying cloud stocks are widely undervalued today." This statement gives a boost to our argument that Amazon.com, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMZN) stock still has tremendous upside left. With AWS being one of the key drivers of AMZN stock, this study suggests AMZN stock is set to gain further, on account of AWS.

How to Play AMZN stock now?

Technicals suggest Amazon stock could retreat in the near-term. If one observes the technical chart of AMZN stock, one can see the stock has entered the overbought territory, as per the 'Bollinger bands' technical indicator. AMZN stock price has presently breached the upper Bollinger band indicating that the stock could see a near-term correction. Another popular technical indicator, Relative Strength Index (RSI), also suggests AMZN stock is very near to overbought territory with an RSI measure of 69.06. 70 is the overbought threshold, as per the RSI measure. AMZN stock has been Wall street's darling over the years in spite of its lofty valuations and question marks over profitability. A Seeking Alpha earnings preview further suggests Amazon (NASDAQ:AMZN) will beat street expectations for Q1 earnings with a margin of 8.7% and suggests an implied share price of $967, much higher than the last close. AMZN stock still remains a good long-term buy even at these highs and any pullback would present the best opportunity to go long on AMZN stock.
