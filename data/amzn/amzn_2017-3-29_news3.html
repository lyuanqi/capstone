AMZN Stock: Why Amazon.com Inc Stock Could Surge Past $1000

Posted by Kumar Abhishek on March 29, 2017, 06:55 AM EST
Related Ticker: AMZN

Analysts recently upgraded price targets on Amazon.com, Inc stock due to strong eCommerce growth, emerging non-retail business and physical stores.


Flickr

Shares of eCommerce giant Amazon.com, Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) have been on a roll, rising almost 50% over the past year, handsomely beating the S&P 500 (INDX:SPAL) in the process. However, over the past one month, the stock has been trading in a comparatively flat zone, with AMZN stock price hovering around $850 from the beginning of the month. In the previous week, AMZN stock breached the support from the 20-day simple moving average (SMA) after hitting an all-time high of $862.8. Since then the stock has been facing strong resistance from the 20-day SMA. However, Amazon.com Inc stock broke out above the 20-day SMA line in yesterday's trade after finding a strong support at the 50-day SMA. The jump in the price was accompanied by higher volumes. From technical analysis front, Amazon stock will face the next resistance at $862.8, its all-time high.

Source: Amazon.com, Inc technical analysis chart by amigobulls.com

eCommerce growth will continue to drive Amazon.com Inc stock.

While technical analysis is flashing a buy signal for Amazon stock, even the fundamentals are strong for the company. Stifel Nicolaus Analyst Scott Devitt boosted the target price for Amazon stock from $912 to $1025 driven by strong momentum in core eCommerce growth and the emergence of the non-retail business. The new price target indicates almost 20% upside from yesterday's close.

Amazon's eCommerce business will keep its growth momentum going as more and more customers shift to online shopping. According to National Retail Federation, U.S eCoommerce sales will grow by 8%-12% in 2017, almost three times higher than 2.8% growth for traditional brick-and-mortar retailers. According to statista, U.S eCommerce sales are likely to grow at a CAGR of 9.3% till 2020. The recent surge in consumer confidence and rising employment and wages will further improve consumer demand. Being one of the most dominant eCommerce players, Amazon is likely to be the biggest beneficiary of this growth.

The global shift towards eCommerce will be even more pronounced. Global eCommerce sales are expected to grow at a CAGR of 20.7% from 2016 to 2020. According to eMarketer, the share of eCommerce in the total retail sales will increase worldwide, from 8.7% in 2016 to 10% in 2017, an increase of $370 billion in dollar terms. This will further rise to $4.058 trillion or 14.6% of global retail sales by 2020. And Amazon's recent bets in middle east and India will provide Amazon access to fast-growing markets in the world. As we have reported earlier, acquisition of Souq.com will be a catalyst for Amazon stock. Middle east is currently a fertile ground for eCommerce investment. B2C e-commerce share of total retail goods sold in the UAE alone is projected to triple between 2014 and 2019.

Amazon.com, Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) completes a full circle.

Apart from eCommerce growth, another reason why some analysts are bullish on Amazon is because of its growing offline presence. In an irony of sorts, Amazon.com, Inc which made its name by disrupting the brick-and-mortar retail market is planning on more physical presence. According to a New York Times article, Amazon is going to make a strong push in the bookstore, grocery and furniture segments. Recently, Amazon opened its first store in the same mall where the giant Barnes & Noble (NYSE:BKS) had its bookstore before Amazon disrupted the publishing industry. For every store that Barnes and Noble will close this year, Amazon is likely to open a new one. Amazon is also opening more grocery shops and fulfillment centers, including one in India. But given the recent chaos in the brick-and-mortar retail space, why is Amazon.com Inc planning to enter this space?

In spite of strong growth in online shopping, eCommerce is expected to make only 14% of global retail sales by 2020. According to a release from U.S Department of Commerce, eCommerce made up only 9.3% of total sales in the fourth quarter of 2016. The majority of retail sales still occur in traditional brick-and-mortar stores. While the competition will be intense in the physical retail space, Amazon is banking on the use of technology to give it an edge. Also, more physical stores will help Amazon fill its internet orders more quickly.

Apart from strong eCommerce growth and physical stores, the emergence of non-retail segments like AWS and payments will also act as tailwinds. Amazon also has a fast-growing advertising business which is expected to have generated $1.2 billion in revenues in 2016. And given the tons of data Amazon has about customers shopping styles, this business is likely to grow at a rapid pace. Another growth area for Amazon.com Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) is the rise of Voice-Activated Internet. RBC Capital Markets’ analysts Mark Mahaney and Jim Shaughnessy argue that Alexa and Echo devices will contribute up to $10 billion in revenues by 2020, up from a negligible amount now.

Amazon.com, Inc remains a good long-term buy.

In spite of high valuations and low profits, Amazon.com, Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) stock has been a favorite on Wall Street for a long time. And the stock has lived up to the expectations, returning more than 2000% in the last ten years. Analysts continue to remain bullish about AMZN stock. Barclays initiated coverage of Amazon stock with an Overweight rating and $1,120 price target, 30% upside from yesterday's close. 89% of analysts tracking AMZN stock rate it a "buy" or better, with an average 12-month price target of $941.38. Given the strong growth in eCommerce and other growth drivers, AMZN stock remains a good long-term buy.
