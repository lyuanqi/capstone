Stocks Higher As S&P 500 Reaches Key Level, Amazon Hits New High

  • JUAN CARLOS ARANCIBIA
  • 9/22/2016
Reprints

The stock market came off session highs Thursday afternoon, but remains solidly higher, as energy and technology stocks shouldered much of the day's leadership.

In one of the most significant moves, the S&P 500 climbed 0.5% and is back above the 50-day moving average. The possibility that the index could get bogged below that price indicator had been a concern, but today's rise lessens that worry.

The Nasdaq climbed 0.6%, hitting a record high intraday. The Dow Jones industrial average added 0.5%, finding resistance at the 50-day line. All indexes were at or near session highs. Winning stocks led losers overwhelmingly.

Technology stocks were among the Stocks On The Move, including e-commerce, semiconductor and internet companies. Amazon.com (AMZN) rose 1.5% to a new high in volume that was among the busiest in today's trading. The stock is extended from a pullback to the 50-day moving average.

Match Group (MTCH), the parent company of dating website Match.com, broke out of a choppy base. Shares rose past the 17.10 buy point in ample volume. This week, the company's Tinder app said it will let users blend music from streaming service Spotify into their profiles.

Match Group went public nearly a year ago at 12 a share. It is one of 12 stocks in the internet content industry group (which has 57 stocks) with Composite Ratings of 95 or better. Match Group owns more than 45 online dating and social brands, plus the Princeton Review college rating service.

Energy and metals stocks led the market. West Texas intermediate crude rose more than 2% to $46.40 a barrel. Gold, silver and copper were up 1% to 2%.

Papa John's International (PZZA) jumped nearly 4% in heavy trading. The stock had already edged past a 78.19 buy point but now has asserted itself above that entry. It remains in buy range. Rival Domino's Pizza (DPZ) also hit a new high as it climbs from support at the 10-week moving average.

A much less satisfying breakout came from Steris (STE). The maker of infection prevention and other medical equipment poked above the 74.55 buy point of a cup-shaped base. But the stock dipped back below the entry and volume was running significantly below normal levels.
