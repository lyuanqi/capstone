FANG Stocks: Google Undercuts Buy Point; Amazon, Facebook Test Theirs

  • PATRICK SEITZ
  • 1/30/2017
Reprints

XAutoplay: On | Off

The closely watched FANG stocks (Facebook (FB), Amazon (AMZN), Netflix (NFLX) and Google parent Alphabet (GOOGL)) took a beating Monday along with the broader market. But Alphabet suffered the biggest hit, plummeting below its recent buy point.

Two other stocks in the group, Facebook and Amazon, tested their buy points on the stock market today. Many investors add Apple (AAPL) to this list for a FANG-Plus group of tech leaders.

The Nasdaq fell 0.8%, while the Dow Jones industrial average and S&P 500 each shed roughly 0.6% on Monday. Small-cap stocks lagged, with the Russell 2000 shedding more than 1.3%. Volume was higher across the board vs. Friday.

RELATED:

Is Trump Rally Over? Dow Gives Up 20,000; Chevron, Exxon, Alphabet Thumped

Alphabet

Alphabet tumbled 2.5% to 823.83 on Monday, undercutting its 824.40 buy point, which it crossed on Jan. 6. It had been down as much as 2.9% intraday.

On Friday, Alphabet fell 1.4%.

Alphabet had its price target raised by Canaccord Genuity, to 950 from 925, with a buy rating. Alphabet reported fourth-quarter results late Thursday with the Mountain View, Calif.-based company missing views on earnings per share but topping expectations on revenue.

Facebook

Facebook stock sank 0.9% to 130.98 on Monday. Intraday it had been down as much as 2%.

Shares remain in a buy zone from their breakout last week at a 129.37 buy point. Shares approached but did not undercut that buy point on Monday.

The social networking giant is scheduled to report fourth-quarter earnings after the market close Wednesday.

RELATED:

What To Look For When Facebook Reports Q4 Earnings

Amazon.com

E-commerce leader Amazon.com saw its stock undercut a recent buy point of 821.75 in intraday trading. But Amazon ended Monday above that buy point, down 0.6% to 830.38.

Seattle-based Amazon is scheduled to report fourth-quarter earnings on Thursday after the market close.

RELATED:

Amazon's Stellar Holiday Season Will Offset Continued Heavy Spending

Netflix

Internet television network Netflix saw its stock fall 0.9% to 141.22 on Monday. But the stock is still just off its all-time high of 143.46, reached on Jan. 19 after it reported better-than-expected Q4 results.

Netflix stock broke out of a cup-with-handle base on Jan. 4, clearing a buy point of 129.39.

RELATED:

5 Key Takeaways From Netflix's Terrific Q4 Earnings Report

Apple

Apple stock fell 0.3% to 121.63 on Monday. Investors were in a holding pattern ahead of the Cupertino, Calif.-based company's fiscal first-quarter report, due out late Tuesday.

Apple stock broke out of a cup-with-handle chart pattern on Jan. 9, clearing a buy point of 118.12. It has remained in the buy zone since then.
