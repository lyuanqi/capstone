Apple, Amazon And Intel - Today's Technical Stock Trading Ideas

Posted by Vikram Nagarkar on April 04, 2017, 06:43 AM EST
Related Tickers: AAPL , AMZN , INTC

Apple Inc (NASDAQ:AAPL), Amazon.com Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) and Intel Corporation (NASDAQ:INTC) are today's technical stock trading ideas.

AAPL Stock Has Entered Overbought Territory.

Shares of Cupertino, California-based Apple Inc (NASDAQ:AAPL) have had a good run so far in 2017. However, AAPL stock could be headed for a short term correction anytime soon. A look at Apple's technical chart shows that Apple's stock price has hit the upper Bollinger Band, and that's generally a bearish signal, indicating an overbought condition. With the bands coming closer together, one could expect to see more volatility going forward. And if you go by other signals, like the Relative Strength Index (RSI), this volatility to lead to a move lower. The RSI is currently 69.9, just short of 70, which is a commonly used threshold to signify an overbought condition.

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) offers no real assistance, with the MACD and signal line so close to each other over several preceding sessions. A look at volumes also reinforces the possibility of a correction, given that the recent upticks have all come on the back of declining volumes. AAPL stock (NASDAQ:AAPL) has gained by over 24% so far in 2017, and the stock could now be due for a near term correction, which could be used as a buying opportunity by long term investors.

AMZN Stock - Bullish MACD Crossover And Break Out Above 20 Day Moving Average.

 

Like in the case of Apple, shares of Seattle, Washington-based Amazon.com Inc (NASDAQ:AMZN) have also had a good run in 2017, rising by over 18% in the Year To Date (YTD). And at this point, Amazon's technical charts can be a tad confusing. If you go by the RSI or the Bollinger Bands, both indicators suggest that the stock is in overbought territory. While Amazon's stock price has clearly risen above the upper Bollinger Band, the RSI reading now stands above 76, both of which point to an overbought condition. However, AMZN stock may have some steam left before a correction comes through. For starters, AMZN shares shot above its 20 day Simple Moving Average (SMA) on strong volumes, towards the end of the previous month. And the MACD, which made a bullish crossover around the same time will likely reinforce this uptrend in the near term.

INTC Stock Makes Bullish Crossover Backed By Strong Volumes.

Contrary to the other two cases we discussed in this post, shares of Santa Clara, California-based Intel Corporation (NASDAQ:INTC) have been in a downtrend for a while now - since the end of January this year, to be precise. Naturally, both, the stock's 20 day SMA and 50 day SMA have been falling, with the 50 day SMA being the indicator of the longer term trend between the two. Intel's stock price rose past its 20 day moving average towards the end of March. And if you pull up Intel's technical chart as on today, you'll notice how Intel's stock price rallied past its 50 day moving average yesterday, suggesting that a short-term rally could be on the cards. What's more, Intel's MACD has also made a bullish crossover just days prior to yesterday's uptick. So, even though the stock looks overbought based on the RSI and Bollinger Bands, a short term rally looks quite likely for the Chipzilla.
