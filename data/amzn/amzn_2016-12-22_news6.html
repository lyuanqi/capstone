Amazon Primed For Growth on FedEx Earnings Miss

December 22, 2016, 08:13:53 AM EDT By Richard Saintvilus

Shutterstock photo

Shares of FedEx (FDX) got punished Wednesday, falling as much as 3.4% to a session low of $192.04 on heavy volume after the worldwide transportation giant failed to deliver the earnings beat investors expected for its fiscal second quarter. But FedEx's loss can be Amazon's (AMZN) gain, if investors are paying attention.

For the quarter that ended November, FedEx posted adjusted earnings of $2.80 per share on revenues of $14.9 billion. While the revenue total was inline with consensus estimates of $14.92 billion, the Memphis, Tenn.-based company missed the Street's bottom line target of $2.91 per share by 11 cents. The management blamed the earnings miss on a combination of network expansion and weak profits in its bread-and-butter freight business.

However, Fedex also enjoyed solid saw growth in its ground package segment, which produced a five-percentage point increase in package volume. FedEx attributed this growth to a rapid rise and growth in its e-commerce business. During the conference call with analysts, FedEx management, which projected U.S. and global growth above 2% in 2017 and projected fiscal year EPS guidance of $11.85 to $12.35, was noticeably upbeat about e-commerce.

And when discussing growth in e-commerce, the first name that often comes to mind is Amazon, making AMZN stock a solid play heading into the all-important holiday quarter. Total e-commerce sales across the globe will reach $22.04 trillion this year, up 6.0% year over year, according to eMarketer, which estimates sales will top $27 trillion in 2020. And the firm, which cites expansion into new categories such as groceries, expects e-commerce sales in North America will rise 15.6% this year to reach $423.34 billion.

Earlier this month Amazon launched its grocery store concept called Amazon Go, which is currently being tested by Amazon employees in Seattle, boasting the slogan: “No Lines. No Checkouts.” Amazon Go uses a combination of computer vision, deep learning algorithms, and sensor fusion to create what it calls - “Just Walk Out Technology.”

It would seem, between FedEx's confidence with e-commerce and eMarketer's growth forecasts, Amazon -- which is now squeezing melons -- stands to outmuscle its competitors. And to say nothing about last week's U.S. consumer spending report, showing a 15% spike in online sales. So, now's the time to go shopping for AMZN stock, which is down 2.5% in three months, but a sure bet to reach $900 in 2017, delivering 15% to 20% returns.
